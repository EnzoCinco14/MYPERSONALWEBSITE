My Life Journey

Childhood Years

When I was a kid, life was pretty simple and fun. Most of the time, I’d hang out at computer shops, playing video games with my friends. It was such an exciting time, and I felt like I had all the time in the world to just enjoy myself. But it wasn’t all about the computer games. When I wasn’t playing inside the house, I was outside with my friends, running around and just being a kid. We’d play sports or just mess around, and I remember feeling so free. I loved those moments, and they made me happy every single day. One of the best parts of my childhood was spending special days with my family. Whether it was a simple dinner out or a bigger celebration, I always felt excited to be with them. They were such a big part of my happiness, and I loved being around them. I also had a lot of friends growing up, and we were always together. There was something comforting about having so many people to laugh and share memories with. Honestly, I don’t even remember worrying about anything back then. Life just felt light and easy, and I was always surrounded by laughter. I didn’t need anything else to feel happy. The days were full of adventures, even if it was just going out to play or hanging with my family. I think that’s why I look back at my childhood so fondly it was simple, but it was exactly what I needed. I remember just being happy all the time, without a care in the world. Looking back, I feel so grateful for all those small but special moments that really shaped who I am today.

Teenage Years

My teenage years were definitely a mix of figuring things out and having fun. I started to experience things I hadn’t before like drinking with my friends. It wasn’t always about partying or getting drunk, but more about just hanging out, trying new things, and feeling like I was part of the group. Of course, there were times when it got a little wild, but I always tried to keep things in check. What kept me grounded though was school. Even though I was out there having my fair share of teenage experiences, I still cared about my studies. APEC Schools played a huge role in helping me stay focused. The teachers were cool, and they really cared about how we did. They pushed me to do my best, even when I felt like I wanted to be anywhere else but in class. It was tough at times, juggling school work and all the distractions that come with being a teenager, but I managed. I remember pulling all-nighters to finish assignments, but still, I'd find time to hang out with my friends afterward, laughing at the most random stuff. We’d talk about everything in life, school, dreams, and even the dumb stuff we did the night before. Sometimes, I’d get lost in those conversations, thinking about what I wanted for my future. I wasn’t perfect, and yeah, I made my mistakes, but they taught me a lot. I learned how to balance fun with responsibility, which wasn’t always easy. But I always tried to stay true to myself, no matter what. It was hard sometimes, feeling torn between the pressures of school and wanting to enjoy my teenage years. But those years taught me how to manage my time better, and I definitely grew up a lot. I was figuring out who I really was and learning how to handle situations I’d never faced before. By the end of high school, I had a clearer idea of who I was becoming. Teenage years are always a little chaotic, but looking back, they were some of the most important years in shaping who I am today.

College Years

College has been a whole new world for me, and I’m really starting to feel the difference from high school. I’m currently in my second year as an IT student, and let me tell you, it’s been both exciting and overwhelming. The subjects are so much more complicated, and there’s a lot to learn coding, systems, networks but I’m slowly starting to get the hang of it. At first, I honestly felt lost with some of the lessons, and I wondered if I could keep up. But with time, I started to enjoy the challenge. It’s a lot of late-night study sessions and figuring things out on my own, but it feels rewarding when I finally crack something that seemed impossible. One thing I’ve realized is that college is more than just about passing exams. It’s about learning how to solve real-world problems and apply what I’m studying to actual situations. It’s been tough juggling schoolwork, projects, and exams, but I’m getting better at managing my time and staying organized. I’m also lucky to have classmates who are just as passionate about IT, and we’ve helped each other through tough assignments. There are definitely days when I feel frustrated, but I remind myself that it’s all part of the process. College isn’t just about academics but also about building character, resilience, and learning how to bounce back from setbacks. Sometimes, I get a little anxious about the future, but I know that all the effort I’m putting in now will pay off in the long run. The more I learn, the clearer it becomes that this is what I want to do for a living. Being part of this field feels exciting because technology is always evolving, and there’s always something new to explore. I’ve also met some really great people along the way who share the same goals, and that makes everything a bit more fun. I won’t lie, it’s been tough at times, but I know I’m learning more than just technical skills. I’m learning how to be patient, persistent, and determined. As I continue my journey as an IT student, I’m feeling more confident about the future and what’s ahead. I still have a long way to go, but I’m excited for what’s to come. College might be challenging, but it’s also an adventure, and I’m ready to take it on.
